Travelogy

See what's trending in 2017

Havana, Cuba

Vamos a Cuba! The Habanos Festival runs from late-February to early-March and celebrates the cigar culture of the island.

Reykjavik, Iceland

Though one of the oldest art fests in Europe, the Reykjavik Arts Festival brings cutting-edge art to the island every year from May to June.

Auckland, New Zealand

For 10 days, the annual Pride Festival in February brings the community together in celebration of life and love.

Bali, Indonesia

The Makepung Buffalo Races in July are a different sort of running with the bulls. The tradition is to race chariots around harvested paddy fields.

Tokyo, Japan

Japan's infatuation with cartoon animals is reinforced by the recently-opened (and instantly popular) Snoopy Museum.

Cartagena, Colombia

One of the biggest rumbas of the year is Storyland Cartagena, a 3-day festival in January featuring the world's biggest DJs.

Lisbon, Portugal

Festas de Lisboa keep the party going for the entire month of June with new reasons to celebrate almost every day.

Medellín, Colombia

La Feria de Las Flores is a Medellín attraction that celebrates the natural beauty of Colombia every August, with parties every night.

Calgary, Canada

In celebration of culture and pyrotechnics, GlobalFest in August is a music festival-and-fireworks hybrid.

Sydney, Australia

August's City2Surf is 9-mile run from city center to Bondi Beach and draws over 75,000 participants every year.
